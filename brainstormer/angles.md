# Audience Profile — STARS 2026

## Primary Persona: The *Stars!* Veteran

**Who:** Patrick. Late-30s+ strategy gamer who played the original *Stars!* in the late 90s / early 2000s. Has tried to get it running on modern Windows, hit `winevdm` walls, given up. Owns a modern PC, a phone, occasionally a tablet. Knows what a PRT is. Knows what Inner Strength means without looking it up. Has *opinions* about HE vs IS vs CA matchups.

**Pain:**
- Cannot run the original on current hardware without arcane setup.
- Existing clones (craig-stars, Stars Nova) feel incomplete or stalled.
- No mobile version exists at all.
- PBEM games used to take *weeks* — modern life doesn't have that patience.
- Wants the game on his phone for hotel rooms, doctor's waiting rooms, lunch breaks.

**Wants:**
- The exact gameplay he remembers, not a "spiritual successor" that changed the math.
- A modern UI that respects his time (search, sort, filter, undo).
- Async multiplayer that doesn't punish him for having a job.
- Determinism — when he loses a battle, he wants to know it wasn't an RNG glitch.
- Beautiful pixel art that honors the 1995 aesthetic without looking like a museum piece.

**Doesn't want:**
- Microtransactions.
- A "free-to-play" hook.
- An AI that cheats.
- A real-time-mode bolted on.
- Anything that smells like a mobile gacha.

## Secondary Persona: The 4X Curious

**Who:** Younger strategy gamer (20s–30s) who has played *Civ*, *Stellaris*, *Distant Worlds*, maybe *Master of Orion*, but never *Stars!*. Has heard it whispered about as "the deepest 4X ever made." Curious. Skeptical of a 1995 UI.

**Pain:**
- Modern 4X games feel either too shallow (mobile) or too sprawling (Stellaris late game).
- Wants depth without bloat.

**Wants:**
- A clean tutorial that explains PRTs without a 200-page manual.
- A UI that doesn't look like Windows 3.1.
- A reason to care about the racial design phase.

## Tertiary Persona: The Multiplayer Group

**Who:** A group of 4–16 friends who used to play PBEM together, scattered across timezones now. They don't want to host a Stars AutoHost server. They want a link, a deadline, and notifications.

**Pain:**
- Coordinating turn submission across timezones.
- One player going dark and stalling the whole game.
- Setting up email automation in 2026 is absurd.

**Wants:**
- One link to join.
- Deadlines that auto-resolve.
- AI takeover when someone vanishes.
- Push notifications.

## Anti-Persona (NOT the audience)

- Casual mobile gamer wanting 5-minute sessions.
- Real-time strategy fans (this is turn-based, hard stop).
- Players who want loot boxes or progression unlocks.
- Anyone who thinks "abandonware" means "fair game to monetize."

## Voice & Tone for the Project

- Reverent toward the original, not slavish.
- Technical and precise — this audience knows what "minelayer" and "starbase upgrade refund" mean.
- No marketing fluff. No "revolutionary." No "next-gen."
- Show, don't tell. Screenshots > adjectives.
